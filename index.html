What Is Deionized Water?

Deionized water — also known as DI water, demineralized water, or demin water — is water that has been purified through an ion-exchange process to remove virtually all dissolved mineral salts. This includes positively charged ions (cations) like sodium, calcium, magnesium, and iron, as well as negatively charged ions (anions) such as chloride, sulfate, and bicarbonate.

The deionization process works by passing water through specially manufactured resins. Cation resins exchange hydrogen ions (H+) for dissolved mineral cations, while anion resins exchange hydroxyl ions (OH−) for dissolved anions. The released H+ and OH− ions combine to form pure water (H₂O). In mixed-bed deionization, both resin types are combined in a single column to achieve the highest purity levels — up to 18.2 MΩ·cm resistivity, which represents theoretically pure water.

Why resistivity matters: Pure water resists electrical current because it lacks the dissolved ions that carry charge. The higher the resistivity, the fewer ions remain. ASTM Type 1 water at 18.2 MΩ·cm is as close to ion-free as commercially achievable — making it essential for sensitive analytical instruments like HPLC, GC-MS, and ICP systems where even trace minerals can interfere with results.

Unlike distilled water, which relies on evaporation and condensation (and may still contain volatile organic compounds), deionization specifically targets ionic contaminants with greater efficiency and throughput. When combined with additional purification steps like reverse osmosis, UV oxidation, and sub-micron filtration, the result is ultrapure water meeting the strictest ASTM D1193 specifications.

For everyday consumers, deionized water offers practical benefits too. Aquarium hobbyists use DI water to create a mineral-free baseline for precise water chemistry control. Auto detailers rely on it for spot-free final rinses. CPAP users prevent mineral scale in their humidifier chambers. And battery technicians top off lead-acid cells without introducing contaminants that shorten battery life.

Frequently Asked Questions About Deionized Water

Everything you need to know before buying DI water online.

What is deionized water?

Deionized water (DI water) is water that has had virtually all of its mineral ions removed through a process called ion exchange. This includes cations like sodium, calcium, and magnesium, and anions like chloride and sulfate. The result is extremely pure water suitable for laboratory, industrial, and consumer applications where mineral contamination must be avoided.

What is deionized water used for?

Deionized water is used in laboratories for reagent preparation and instrument calibration, in aquariums and reef tanks to create mineral-free water, in CPAP humidifier chambers to prevent mineral buildup, in auto detailing for spot-free rinses, in lead-acid batteries to avoid contamination, and in hydroponics for precise nutrient mixing.

What is the difference between deionized water and distilled water?

Distilled water is purified by boiling and condensing steam, which removes minerals and many contaminants. Deionized water is purified through ion-exchange resins that specifically target mineral ions. DI water can achieve higher purity levels (up to 18.2 MΩ·cm resistivity) and is produced more efficiently than distillation. For most applications, ASTM-grade deionized water meets or exceeds distilled water purity.

Is deionized water safe to drink?

Deionized water is not toxic and is safe in small amounts, but it is not intended as drinking water. Because it lacks minerals, it has a flat taste and does not provide the electrolytes found in regular drinking water. DI water is designed for laboratory, industrial, and specialty applications where mineral-free water is required.

What do ASTM Type 1 and Type 2 mean?

ASTM D1193 is the standard that classifies reagent water into purity grades. Type 1 (Ultrapure) has the highest purity at 18.2 MΩ·cm resistivity with less than 10 ppb TOC — used for critical analytical work like HPLC and PCR. Type 2 (Pure) has greater than 1.0 MΩ·cm resistivity with less than 50 ppb TOC — ideal for general lab use, aquariums, batteries, CPAP machines, and auto detailing.
